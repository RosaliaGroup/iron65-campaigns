Iron65 vs 55 Union: Honest Newark Apartment Comparison

When searching for luxury apartments in Newark, two properties consistently appear at the top of renters' lists: Iron65 and 55 Union. Both offer modern amenities and convenient NYC commutes, but they cater to different lifestyles and budgets. This comprehensive comparison breaks down everything from pricing and amenities to neighborhood dynamics, helping you make an informed decision about your next home in Newark.

Location and Neighborhood Comparison

Iron65, located at 65 McWhorter Street, sits in the heart of Newark's historic Ironbound District. This vibrant neighborhood is renowned for its authentic Portuguese and Spanish restaurants, family-owned bakeries, and strong sense of community. The Ironbound offers a genuine cultural experience with Ferry Street's bustling commercial corridor just minutes away.

55 Union, positioned on Union Street in Downtown Newark, provides a more urban business district atmosphere. While both locations offer excellent access to transportation, the downtown area tends to be quieter in the evenings and weekends compared to the consistently lively Ironbound District.

Pricing and Floor Plan Options

Iron65 offers transparent pricing with studios starting from $2,388, Studio Flex units from $2,499, 1BR Flex from $2,650, traditional 1BR from $2,788, and luxury lofts from $3,788. This new construction building, completed in 2024, provides 135 units across 11 stories with consistent modern finishes throughout.

55 Union's pricing varies more significantly based on floor and specific unit features, with studios typically ranging from approximately $2,200 to $2,800 and one-bedrooms from roughly $2,600 to $3,500. However, availability and specific pricing at 55 Union can fluctuate more frequently due to their varied unit types and lease terms.

The key advantage of Iron65's pricing structure is its predictability and the fact that you're getting brand-new construction with the latest building systems and energy efficiency standards.

Amenities and Building Features

Iron65 distinguishes itself with wellness-focused amenities including a rooftop gym, dedicated yoga studio, cold plunge, and saunas—amenities typically found in much more expensive Manhattan buildings. The rooftop terrace offers stunning NYC skyline views, complemented by an outdoor kitchen perfect for entertaining.

Additional Iron65 amenities include 24-hour live security, in-unit washer/dryer, quartz countertops throughout, high-speed fiber internet, package lockers, and on-site parking. The game room provides social spaces for residents, while the building's modern design maximizes natural light in all units.

55 Union offers more traditional luxury amenities including a fitness center, resident lounge, and rooftop access. However, the building lacks the wellness-focused features like cold plunge and dedicated yoga spaces that set Iron65 apart. The older construction also means some units may have varying finish quality and layouts.

Commute and Transportation Access

Both buildings offer excellent NYC commute options, but Iron65 provides more precise timing data. From Iron65, it's an 8-10 block walk (approximately 10 minutes) to Newark Penn Station, offering 20-minute rides to Midtown Manhattan via NJ Transit. PATH train access provides 22-minute commutes to World Trade Center, while Newark Liberty International Airport is just 5 minutes away.

55 Union offers similar transit access with slightly different walking distances to various stations. The downtown location may provide marginally quicker access to some NJ Transit lines, though the difference is typically just 2-3 minutes.

A significant financial advantage for both locations is avoiding NYC income tax as New Jersey residents—savings of approximately $3,000-$9,000 annually depending on income level. Additionally, using NJ Transit instead of driving reduces carbon emissions by up to 69% according to official NJ Transit data.
